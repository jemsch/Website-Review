Website Review

Summary

We calculated the averages of the five strata per website to find tha average "goodness" of a website. Check out our results below:

Facebook
Because of Facebook’s immense popularity, straightforward design, and diverse user base, the site deserves a high grade. However, the site’s lack of security cannot be ignored. Nonetheless, Facebook’s final rating is B+.
Overall Rating: B+
Youtube
Much like Facebook, Youtube has a large and diverse user base and is quite well designed, but it’s lack of security reduces it’s otherwise score. We have concluded that Youtube’s final rating is a B+.
Overall Rating: B+
Reddit
Despite past breaches of security, Reddit is home to a diverse user base, overall okay design, and is fairly popular, giving it a B.
Overall Rating: B
Tumblr
While Tumblr has a fairly strong political bias and a small user base, this low score is made up by an amazing design and adequate security. Tumbr’s final grade is a B.
Overall Rating: B
Twitter
Twitter has a vast and diverse user base and an amazing design, yet has suffered major security breaches in the past. To this, we have determined that Twitter’s final score is a B.
Overall Rating: B
HuffPost
Despite it’s mediocre design and high security, HuffPo has a smallish user base and is chock full of left-wing political bias, warranting a final grade of C+.
Overall Rating: C+
The Daily Wire
The Daily Wire has an okay design and quite a good security policy, yet it suffers from stifling right-wing political bias and a very small user base. The Daily Wire’s final grade is a C+.
Overall Rating: C+
